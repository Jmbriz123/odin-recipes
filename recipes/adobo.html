Chicken Adobo Recipes

Description

Chicken adobo is a classic Filipino dish made by simmering chicken in a savory-sour sauce of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. It’s known for its rich, bold flavor and tender meat, often served with rice. This dish is a staple in Filipino households and showcases the country's love for simple yet flavorful cooking.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (usually bone-in, skin-on parts like thighs or drumsticks)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2–3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns (or cracked pepper)
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (optional, for a slightly sweet touch)
  • 1 medium onion, sliced (optional, regional variations)
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil (optional, for sautéing)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Boiled egg or potatoes (optional additions)

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken

    2. In a bowl, combine chicken pieces with soy sauce, vinegar, crushed garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for better flavor).

  2. Sear the chicken (optional but recommended)

    3. Heat oil in a pan and brown the chicken pieces on both sides. This adds extra flavor and texture to the meat.

  3. Simmer the chicken

    4. Pour in the marinade, then add water to help cook the chicken through. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for 30–40 minutes until the chicken is tender.

  4. Adjust the flavor

    5. Taste the sauce. If needed, adjust by adding more vinegar for extra tang, or a bit of sugar to balance the flavors.

  5. Reduce the sauce

    6. Let it simmer uncovered to reduce the sauce to your desired thickness. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.

  6. Serve hot

    7. Serve with steamed rice, and optionally top with boiled eggs or a few chili slices for a spicy kick.
