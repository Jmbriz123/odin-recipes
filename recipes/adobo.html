Chicken Adobo Recipes

Description

Chicken adobo is a classic Filipino dish made by simmering chicken in a savory-sour sauce of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. It’s known for its rich, bold flavor and tender meat, often served with rice. This dish is a staple in Filipino households and showcases the country's love for simple yet flavorful cooking.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (usually bone-in, skin-on parts like thighs or drumsticks)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2–3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns (or cracked pepper)
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (optional, for a slightly sweet touch)
  • 1 medium onion, sliced (optional, regional variations)
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil (optional, for sautéing)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Boiled egg or potatoes (optional additions)

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and bay leaves for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for more flavor).
  2. Sauté garlic and onions in oil (optional step for deeper flavor).
  3. Add the marinated chicken (including the marinade) into a pan. Add water, peppercorns, and sugar if using.
  4. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer uncovered for about 30–40 minutes until chicken is tender and sauce is slightly reduced.
  5. Optional: Remove the chicken, fry it for a crispy skin, then return it to the sauce.
  6. Taste and adjust salt, sugar, or vinegar based on preference.
  7. Serve with steamed rice.
