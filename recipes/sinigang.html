Pork Sinigang Recipes

Description

Pork Sinigang is a classic Filipino sour soup made with tender pork (usually belly or ribs) simmered in a tangy broth flavored with tamarind. It’s often cooked with vegetables like kangkong (water spinach), sitaw (long beans), labanos (radish), and eggplant. Known for its comforting, appetizing sourness, sinigang is a beloved Filipino dish often enjoyed with steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork (preferably belly, ribs, or shoulder), cut into chunks
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 pack tamarind soup base (e.g., Knorr Sinigang Mix) or fresh tamarind, boiled and mashed
  • 6 cups water
  • 1–2 tbsp fish sauce (patis), to taste
  • 1 medium labanos (radish), sliced
  • 1 cup sitaw (long beans), cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 1–2 eggplants, sliced
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach) or pechay
  • 2 pcs green chili (siling haba), optional
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Boil the pork

    2. In a large pot, place the pork and add water just enough to cover. Bring to a boil, then skim off the scum/foam that rises to the top.

  2. Add aromatics

    3. Add the sliced onions and tomatoes. Lower the heat, cover, and simmer for 45–60 minutes, or until the pork is tender.

  3. Add the vegetables

    4. Add the labanos (radish) and cook for about 5 minutes. Then add the eggplant and sitaw (long beans) and continue simmering for another 5–7 minutes.

  4. Add the souring agent

    5. Pour in the sinigang mix (or fresh tamarind extract if using). Stir and adjust the sourness to your taste.

  5. Season

    6. Add fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. You may also add green chili (siling haba) for mild heat.

  6. Add greens

    7. Finally, add kangkong (water spinach) or pechay. Cook for about 1–2 minutes until wilted.

  7. Serve hot

    8. Serve with steamed rice and enjoy the warm, tangy broth.
